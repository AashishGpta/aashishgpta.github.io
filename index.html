About

Let me introduce myself.

I am an Astronomy Ph.D. student in European Southern Observatory (ESO), Garching (near Munich), Germany, working with Dr. Anna Miotello. My thesis project here focuses on systematically studying infall of material, especially through streamers, onto planet-forming disks in Class II stage.

I did my bachelor's in Mechanical Engineering at IIITDM Jabalpur, India. However, those things lacked a certain gravity to me (pun intended) and I discovered my love for astronomy. I did a few online courses, a couple of internships at ARIES, India, went to NCU, Taiwan for my master's, another internship at ASIAA, Taiwan, and now I am here.

Besides astronomy, I enjoy reading random books, cooking (mostly Indian food), swimming in natural water bodies, pretending to play flute, and exploring more of our beautiful planet.

TIPSY stands for Trajectory of Infalling Particles in Streamers around Young stars. It's an open source code for dyanmical analysis of molecular-line observations of trails of infalling gas around protostars.

To systematically look for Class II sources accreting material form surrounding clouds, we demonstrate that reflection nebulae can be used to identify such sources.

Details Close

For a sample of 32 protostars, we analyzed motion of gas using molecular-line data obtained with the Submillimeter Array (SMA) and the magnetic field structures using polarimetric data obtained with the James Clerk Maxwell Telescope (JCMT). We investigated the effects of the core-scale magnetic field structures on envelope-scale gas kinematics.

Details Close

We use the deep 850 μm image of the core of Rho Ophiuchus generated as part of the JCMT Transient Survey, to trace dusty molecular gas. On the other hand, we also compiled a catalogue of YSOs in the region using published lists in literature and some more members, identified with the Gaia/DR2 data. Using these datasets, we diagnosed the spatial and kinematic correlation of the young stellar population, at different evolutionary stages, with respect to the gaseous structures.
